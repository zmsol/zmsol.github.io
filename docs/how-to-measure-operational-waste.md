# How to Measure Operational Waste in Your Consulting Firm

**You know something is costing you time. You're not sure what or how much.**

Most professional services firms run on a mix of spreadsheets, email threads, sticky notes, and "we've always done it this way." The waste isn't visible because nobody's measuring it. This doc gives you a framework to find it in 30 minutes.

---

## The Three Layers of Operational Waste

### Layer 1: Process Waste (The Obvious)

Tasks that take longer than they should because the process is manual or circuitous.

**Signals:**
- A task that requires opening 3+ different apps or tabs
- A document that gets passed between 2+ people for formatting, not substance
- Data that gets re-entered (client info into a form, then into a spreadsheet, then into an invoice)
- A checklist that lives in someone's head, not on paper

**How to find it:** Pick one repetitive task — client intake, invoice generation, weekly reporting. Time how long one cycle takes from start to finish. Now ask: how much of that time is actual skilled work vs. moving data from one place to another?

### Layer 2: Friction Waste (The Hidden)

Small delays that compound across a week. Each one is 30 seconds. Fifty of them is 25 minutes. Every single day.

**Signals:**
- Searching for a file or email that should be one click away
- Waiting for someone to respond before you can proceed
- Switching between contexts (client A's spreadsheet, client B's email chain, client C's portal)
- Re-creating something you know you've done before because you can't find the template

**How to find it:** For one day, keep a tally every time you stop working to find something, ask something, or switch tools. Count at the end of the day. Multiply by 240 working days.

### Layer 3: Capacity Waste (The Costly)

Senior people doing work that should be done by a system or a junior. This is the most expensive waste in any firm because it's burning billable hours on zero-value activity.

**Signals:**
- A partner or senior consultant creating invoices
- A director formatting documents
- A subject matter expert entering data
- Anyone billing $200+/hour doing work an automated system could do in 30 seconds

**How to find it:** Look at what your most expensive person actually did last week. Assign each task a value tier:

| Tier | Value | What It Looks Like |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Creative | Billable, unique, strategic | Client strategy, expert analysis, high-level decisions |
| Operational | Necessary but repeatable | Reporting, documentation, status updates |
| Mechanical | Zero judgment required | Data entry, formatting, file organization, invoice generation |

If your senior people spend more than 20% of their week in Mechanical tasks, you have capacity waste.

## The 30-Minute Waste Audit

Grab a sheet of paper or open a doc. Answer these five questions:

1. What task does my team complain about most? (That's the one with the highest pain-to-value ratio.)
2. What takes 10+ minutes that should take 2? (Process waste.)
3. What do I search for at least once a day? (Friction waste.)
4. What is my most expensive person doing that a junior (or a system) could do? (Capacity waste.)
5. If I could eliminate one recurring task this month, which one would save the most team hours? (Priority.)

**One hour of mechanical work reclaimed per day = 240 hours per year per person.** At $150/hr billable, that's $36,000 in reclaimed capacity. Per person.
